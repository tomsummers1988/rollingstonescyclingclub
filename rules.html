The Rolling Stones TT Cycling Club Rules

  1. The Rolling Stone Cycle Club is a dictatorsip not a democracy. Dr Tom Blanks word is final and rules above even the Court of Arbitration for Sport
  2. Always blame WiFi, but only once per session. If your avatar is spinning on the spot or your race starts late, it’s the internet’s fault. Repeat offenders must provide GIF-worthy excuses for entertainment value.
  3. The weight you input is the weight you own. Wishful thinking doesn’t make you lighter on Zwift. Keep it real unless you’re prepared for public ridicule.
  4. No burrito googling mid-race. If you have time to search for power-ups, you have time to pull your group back together.
  5. Fan or no fan, no complaints. Whether you’re sweating without airflow or freezing on a turbo in winter, suck it up. Complaining mid-ride is a sign you’re not working hard enough.
  6. Always go “a bit harder” to break an hour. If you’re finishing at 1:01, don’t expect sympathy.
  7. Every ride is a race. Tour de Zwift, KISS, or a meet-up: if there’s a leaderboard, it’s a race. “Not a race” is only said when we’re losing.
  8. Excuses are acceptable, creativity mandatory. From “my gut feels drunk” to “I’ve got sensor issues,” every excuse must be unique. Repeated excuses result in mockery.
  9. The Wattbike always goes in the car first. Your disco, hatchback, or SUV’s first priority is transporting your trainer. Kids, groceries, and even your partner come second.
  10. A group is your best friend. In Zwift races, the draft is king. Drop the group, and you’re riding alone into a virtual void of despair.
  11. Ride with honour: no 45kg avatars. If your weight is exaggeratedly low, we’ll call you out. Uphill flyers are suspect.
  12. There’s no such thing as “too tired.” Whether it’s sweet spot workouts, hill repeats, or another go at Alpe du Zwift, the group expects you to commit.
  13. Chatting mid-ride is pro-level. If you can send WhatsApp messages during intervals, the rest of us will assume you’ve been slacking.
  14. Post-race banter is sacred. Whether you’ve been blown off the back or claimed a glorious top 50 finish, post-race analysis in the group chat is compulsory.
  15. Screenshots or it didn’t happen. No one cares about your FTP bump or epic race effort unless you provide proof. Leaderboard screenshots are gold.
  16. Always cool down—even if it’s downhill. Those free kilometres matter, so spin out your legs after every race. Plus, it’s a good chance to prepare your next excuse.
  17. A neglected family is a sign of progress. Heart rate zones, power outputs, and FTP scores take precedence over bedtime tantrums (but know when to call it).
  18. When in doubt, blame the steering update. If your gears won’t change or your rider veers off course, the update is at fault, not your equipment.
  19. Ringers are welcome, but declare them. Inviting your “mate” with a 4.5W/kg average? Make sure we know they’re secretly Chris Froome.
  20. FTP tests are races against yourself. Treat them with the same intensity as a crit or hill climb. Bonus points for heart rates over 200.
  21. Indoor cyclists have it harder. Real-world riders don’t understand the pain of staring at a screen while pushing watts into the abyss. Let’s remind them when we can.
