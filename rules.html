The Rolling Stones Cycling Club Rules

  1. The Rolling Stone Cycling Club is a dictatorsip not a democracy. Tom Blanks word is final and rules above even the Court of Arbitration for Sport
  2. If in doubt blame the WiFi. If your avatar is spinning on the spot or your race starts late, it’s the internet’s fault.
  3. Fan or no fan, no complaints. Whether you’re sweating without airflow or freezing on a turbo in winter, suck it up. Complaining mid-ride is a sign you’re not working hard enough.
  4. If Sellars is in the race, its draft time. Don't be fooled by the 50 cadence, he's motoring - just remember to nip round him on the line.
  5. Sweat and extension cords don't mix. Neutralising yourself mid ride won't help your teammates.
  6. The Norris quiff and nipple combo are a thing of RSCC legend. Build the legend at every opportunity.
  7. If you get an FTP bump, post it. You earned the praise, bask in the glory.
  8. Every ride is a race. Tour de Zwift, KISS, or a meet-up: if there’s a leaderboard, it’s a race.
  9. Excuses are encouraged but creativity is mandatory. From WiFi to better halves its allowed but mst be unique.
  10. A group is your best friend. In Zwift races, the draft is king. Drop the group, and you’re riding alone into a virtual void of despair.
  11. Ride with honour: no 45kg avatars. If your suspect the AGM scales of truth await.
  12. There’s no such thing as “too tired.” Whether it’s sweet spot workouts, hill repeats, or another go at Alpe du Zwift, the group expects you to commit.
  13. Chatting mid-ride is pro-level. If you can send WhatsApp messages you’re not going hard enough.
  14. Post-race banter is sacred. Whether you’ve been blown off the back or claimed a glorious finish, post-race analysis in the group chat is compulsory.
  15. Screenshots or it didn’t happen. No one cares about your FTP bump or epic race effort unless you provide proof. Leaderboard screenshots are gold.
  16. Always cool down - especially when it’s downhill. Those free kilometres are gold. Plus, it’s a good chance to prepare your next excuse.
  17. Always allow an extra 5 minutes for the Zwift update. Leaving your teammates on the start line is unforgiveable.
  18. Ringers are welcome, the more the merrier. Just make sure they aren't too good.
  19. FTP tests are races against yourself. Treat them with the same intensity as a crit or hill climb. Bonus points for heart rates over 200.
  20. Indoor cyclists have it harder. Real-world riders don’t understand the pain of staring at a screen while pushing watts into the abyss. Let’s remind them when we can.
